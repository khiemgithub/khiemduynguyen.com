The Neoconservative View

In his book “Assassin’s Gate”, George Packer offers his account of the war in Iraq that the United States had participated in, including the developments that took place prior to the its first arrival in this Middle Eastern country.

He began by bringing into discussion the ideas that could likely explain the underlying motives for the U.S.’s decision to take action in Iraq against Saddam Hussein and his brutal regime. Many neoconservatives – with Paul Wolfowitz being the most prominent – firmly believe that eliminating Saddam Hussein’s dictatorship and rule would effectively clean the way for a major restructuring of the Middle East. In this respect, those who share Wolfowitz’s view support an American war against Iraq as they expect the disposal of Hussein and the formation of a new Iraqi national government based on democratic ideals could encourage other states in the region to move away from tyranny and adopt policies that would be more welcome of the western world in general.
