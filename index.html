Inspiration

A few quotes I've collected over the years. Together, they've served as a strong source of inspiration that will constantly remind me to nurture my creativity, imagination, service to the people, and a will to always take the unknown path.

Design is really an act of communication, which means having a deep understanding of the person with whom the designer is communicating.

Don Norman (Co-Founder, Nielsen Norman Group) - from "The Design of Everyday Things"

True simplicity is derived from so much more than just the absences of clutter or ornamentation. It’s about bringing order to complexity.

Jonathan Ive (Chief Design Officer, Apple) - on the new design update for iOS 7

Design is all about finding solutions within constraints. If there were no constraints, it’s not design — it’s art.

Matias Duarte (VP of Design, Google) - on Material Design

Design is the art of solving specific rather than abstract problems. If the problem is not clear or well-understood, you cannot beging to evaluate how effective the solutions are.

Julie Zhuo (VP of Product Design, Facebook) - from "Unintuitive Lessons on Being a Designer"

That combination of thought and action defines creative confidence: the ability to come up with new ideas and the courage to try them out.

David M. Kelley (Founder, IDEO) - from "Creative Confidence"

There is no perfect fit when you're looking for the next big thing to do. You have to take opportunities and make an opportunity fit for you, rather than the other way around. The ability to leanr is the most important quality a leader can have.

Sheryl Sandberg (COO, Facebook) - from "Lean In"

Every user interface should be simplified as much as possible, since every additional feature or item of information on a screen is one more thing to learn, one more thing to possibly misunderstand, and one more thing to search through when looking for the thing you want.

Jakob Nielsen (Co-Founder, Nielsen Norman Group) - from "Usability Engineering"

Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited to all we now know and understand, while imagination embraces the entire world, and all there ever will be to know and understand.

Albert Einstein - on the importance of "imagination"
